Article 38 — Battery Passport

1. From 18 February 2027, industrial batteries with a capacity above 2 kWh, LMT batteries and electric vehicle batteries placed on the market or put into service shall have an electronic record (the 'battery passport') established in accordance with this Article.

2. The battery passport shall be accessible via a data carrier complying with the requirements set out in Annex VI, point 4, and shall contain the information listed in Annex XIII.

3. The economic operator placing the battery on the market or putting it into service shall ensure that the battery passport is kept up to date throughout the life of the battery.

Article 39 — Carbon Footprint

1. From 18 February 2025, electric vehicle batteries placed on the market shall be accompanied by a carbon footprint declaration per kWh of total energy the battery can supply over its expected service life.

2. The carbon footprint shall be calculated in accordance with the methodology established in the delegated act referred to in Article 7(1).

Chapter XIV — Penalties

Member States shall lay down the rules on penalties applicable to infringements of this Regulation and shall take all measures necessary to ensure that they are implemented. The penalties shall be effective, proportionate and dissuasive. Member States shall notify the Commission of those rules and measures and of any subsequent amendments thereto without delay.

ECHA — Candidate List of SVHCs Update (14 January 2025)

The European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) has added the following substances to the Candidate List of Substances of Very High Concern (SVHCs) for authorisation under REACH:

  • UV-327 (CAS 3864-99-1) — persistent, bioaccumulative and toxic (PBT). Used in polymer stabilisers and industrial coatings.
  • Butylparaben (CAS 94-26-8) — endocrine disrupting properties. Used in personal care formulations and industrial coatings.
  • PFHxA (CAS 307-24-4) — persistent, mobile and toxic (PMT). Used in surface treatment and PCB manufacturing.

Suppliers of articles containing these substances above 0.1% w/w must notify ECHA via the SCIP database within 6 months.
